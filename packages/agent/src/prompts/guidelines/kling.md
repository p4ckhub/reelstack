Kling 2.6 prompt guidelines:

BEST AT: dramatic gestures, product interactions, nature close-ups, urban scenes, action moments. Kling 2.6 generates synchronized audio with video (audio-visual choreographer).

WEAK AT: complex multi-person scenes, long continuous shots over 8s, abstract art, in-frame text.

PROMPT RULES:

- Lead with the primary ACTION, not the scene: "a hand slams a coffee cup onto a desk" not "a desk with a coffee cup"
- Short and punchy works best: 20-40 words, one clear moment
- Subject must be unambiguous: "a young woman with dark hair runs through rain" not "a person running"
- Motion verbs drive quality: slam, pour, explode, unfold, sweep, cascade, shatter, ripple
- Style tags that work well: "slow motion", "macro shot" (avoid `cinematic`/`4K` — see prompt-writer.md forbidden list)

EXAMPLE:

```
A barista slams the portafilter into the espresso machine, espresso pours into a small glass cup, steam rises sharply, slow motion, macro lens, single hard top light, dark dramatic background. Audio: metallic clack on impact, hiss of steam.
```

WHEN TO PICK KLING vs ALTERNATIVES:

- vs Seedance: Kling for action / dramatic moments where motion is the entire shot. Seedance for multi-element narrative shots with detailed camera/lighting choreography.
- vs Pika: Kling for higher visual fidelity per shot. Pika for rapid variations and special effects.
- vs Veo 3.1: Kling cheaper, comparable quality on simple action. Veo 3.1 wins on dialogue-driven scenes.

DURATION: 5s or 10s clips. Quality holds well at 5s, degrades past 10s.

ASPECT RATIOS: 9:16, 1:1, 16:9. Kling handles 9:16 well — strong choice for vertical reels.

COST NOTE: ~$0.20-0.50/clip depending on duration and resolution.
