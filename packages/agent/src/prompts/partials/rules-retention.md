## RETENTION EDITING PATTERNS

Algorithmic push kicks in around 75% completion rate. Every second that doesn't earn the next one costs you the next one.

### Pacing rhythm (non-negotiable)

- **0-2s (lock)**: fastest cuts. Typically one opening shot + one immediate visual punch. No breathing room yet.
- **2-10s (payoff setup)**: tight cuts, 1.5-3s each. Keep forward momentum. Any shot ≥4s here will bleed viewers.
- **10-20s (delivery)**: slightly relax, 2-4s shots. Let the viewer absorb a key point. But never more than ONE slow shot in a row.
- **Last 3-5s (kicker)**: burst sequence — short cuts (0.8-1.5s) and energy spike. Counter, zoom, or CTA lands here.

### GOOD vs BAD rhythm

- GOOD: hook(1.5s) → reveal(2s) → demo(3s) → proof-stat(1.5s) → contrast-b-roll(2s) → CTA-burst(3s)
- BAD: intro(4s) → talking-head(5s) → talking-head(6s) → CTA(3s) — 19 seconds of plateau, viewers gone by 7s

### Contrast (the #1 retention weapon)

Viewers don't bail from fast content; they bail from UNIFORM content. Alternate tempo and register:

- Calm talking-head (2-3s) → burst b-roll with effects (rapid 1s cuts)
- Wide shot → aggressive zoom
- Silence → SFX whoosh/impact on key word
- Plain background → glitch/scanline overlay for ONE beat
- Full-screen text card → screenshot with zoom

RULE: every 4-5 seconds something must CHANGE visually — shot type, camera motion, overlay, palette energy. Not just cut to next shot of same kind.

### Beat-aligned cutting

Cut on EMOTIONAL or SEMANTIC beats, not fixed intervals. Listen to the script:

- End of a complete thought? Cut.
- Word of emphasis ("Primo", "73%", "broken")? Shot boundary aligned to that word.
- Rhetorical pause? Hold one extra beat THEN cut — don't cut into the silence.
- Never cut mid-sentence unless it's a rapid-fire list effect.

### Sound-off is default

80-85% of viewers watch without sound (especially first 3s). This means:

- Captions MUST be on screen every second there's narration (pipeline auto-generates them)
- Text-emphasis for hook word is MORE important than voice-over emphasis
- Visual progression must stand on its own — someone watching muted should understand the arc
- SFX is a BONUS, not a crutch. Never rely on a whoosh to carry a beat the visual doesn't

### Retention killers (hard bans)

- Stalling shots: generic "person at laptop" for 4+ seconds
- Same camera angle for 3+ shots in a row (especially talking-head)
- Effects clustered in one section and absent in another (either all dynamic or all calm — never both)
- CTA before 80% mark — viewer didn't earn trust yet, bounces
- Ending on talking-head — last shot must have a VISUAL punch (counter, zoom, CTA card)
