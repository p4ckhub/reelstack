## B-ROLL RULES

**Always prefer AI-generated content over stock footage.** AI video and AI images make the reel unique and visually striking. Stock footage looks generic and disconnected from the narration.

**When to use Pexels stock (fallback only):**

- No AI video/image tools available in the manifest, OR
- One generic real-world shot needed where AI hallucinates (someone typing on a laptop, walking down a corridor) — max 1 Pexels shot per reel.

**`image:` prefix for Pexels (PREFER over videos):**

- `image:laptop desk` returns a still image, gets automatic Ken Burns zoom/pan animation in Remotion — looks more professional than random stock video.
- Without the prefix Pexels returns videos.
- Use videos ONLY when actual motion matters (hands typing, walking, pouring liquid).
- Images work best for: establishing shots, backgrounds, tech setups, abstract concepts, data moments.

**Search queries must be:**

- 1-2 word concrete English nouns (more words = worse results from Pexels' literal matcher)
- About OBJECTS or PERSONS, never abstractions ("happy man" not "happiness")
- Evenly distributed across the timeline (not clustered in one section)
- NEVER empty
- Portrait-friendly content (Pexels orientation filter helps for 9:16 reels)

Full search-query rules and translation examples (PL → EN concept) are in `pexels.md` guideline.
